Building a Supernova Remnant from Spherical Harmonics

This simulation begins with an idealized spherical stellar explosion. The average radius of the remnant is represented by the ℓ = 0 spherical-harmonic mode. Higher-order modes introduce departures from perfect spherical symmetry.

The left panel shows the expanding shock front from t = 0 to an age of 454 years, approximately the present age of Tycho's supernova remnant. The simulation advances at about 150 simulated years per second and freezes when it reaches the present day.

The right panel shows the angular field used to deform the remnant. Increasing the maximum value of ℓ introduces progressively smaller structures on the expanding surface.

Each new explosion uses a different randomized collection of spherical-harmonic coefficients. The same coefficients remain fixed during the animation, allowing one initial disturbance to grow smoothly rather than changing randomly from frame to frame.

Checking Show only current (ℓ, m) isolates one spherical-harmonic component. Unchecking it displays the accumulated superposition of all modes through the selected maximum ℓ.

A nearly spherical explosion can develop a complicated remnant through the superposition of many simple angular modes.
